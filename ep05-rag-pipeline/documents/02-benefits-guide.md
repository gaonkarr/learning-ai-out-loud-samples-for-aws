# PineRidge Solutions — Benefits Guide 2026
**Your Complete Guide to Compensation, Health, and Wellness Benefits**
**Document ID: PR-BEN-2026-018**
**Effective: January 1, 2026 through December 31, 2026**

---

## Eligibility

All regular full-time employees (working 30+ hours per week) are eligible for benefits starting on their first day of employment. Part-time employees working 20–29 hours per week receive prorated benefits as indicated in each section below. Contract and temporary workers are not eligible for benefits.

Benefits elections must be made within your first 14 calendar days of employment. If you miss this window, you must wait until the next annual Open Enrollment period (November 1–15 each year) to enroll, unless you experience a qualifying life event.

## Section 1: Health and Dental Coverage

### 1.1 Health Plan Options

PineRidge offers two health plan tiers through Maplewood Benefits Corp:

**Standard Plan:**
- Monthly premium (employee share): $0 for individual, $85 for employee + spouse, $130 for family
- PineRidge covers 100% of individual premium, 80% of dependent premiums
- Prescription drugs: $5 copay for generic, $25 copay for brand-name (formulary list at benefits.pineridge.internal)
- Paramedical services (physiotherapy, massage, chiropractic, psychology): $75 per session, up to $2,000 per practitioner category per year
- Mental health coverage: 20 sessions per year with a registered psychologist or psychotherapist, covered at $150 per session
- Vision: $300 per person every 24 months for eyeglasses or contact lenses. Eye exams covered annually.
- Out-of-province/country emergency coverage: $5,000,000 lifetime maximum

**Enhanced Plan:**
- Monthly premium (employee share): $45 for individual, $140 for employee + spouse, $210 for family
- PineRidge covers 80% of all premiums (individual and dependent)
- Prescription drugs: $0 copay for generic, $10 copay for brand-name
- Paramedical services: $100 per session, up to $3,500 per practitioner category per year
- Mental health coverage: Unlimited sessions per year at $200 per session
- Vision: $500 per person every 24 months. Laser eye surgery covered up to $2,500 per eye (lifetime)
- Out-of-province/country emergency coverage: Unlimited
- Private hospital room upgrade
- Fertility treatments: up to $15,000 lifetime (IVF, IUI, related medications)
- Gender-affirming care: up to $25,000 lifetime for procedures not covered by provincial health plan

### 1.2 Dental Plan

One dental plan is offered to all eligible employees:

- Monthly premium (employee share): $0 for individual, $40 for family
- Basic services (cleanings, fillings, extractions): 100% covered, two cleanings per year
- Major services (crowns, bridges, dentures): 80% covered, $3,000 annual maximum
- Orthodontics (dependents under 18 only): 50% covered, $4,000 lifetime maximum per dependent
- Dental implants: 80% covered, $5,000 per implant (maximum 2 implants per calendar year)
- Emergency dental (accidents): 100% covered up to $1,000 per incident, no annual maximum

### 1.3 Health Spending Account (HSA)

In addition to your chosen plan, every employee receives a Health Spending Account:

- Annual allocation: $1,000 for individual, $1,500 for employee + spouse, $2,000 for family
- Covers expenses not fully paid by your plan (copays, services beyond plan limits, etc.)
- Can also be used for: ergonomic equipment for home office (up to $500), gym memberships, fitness classes, personal training
- Unused balance carries forward for one year only. Amounts older than 12 months expire.
- Claims must be submitted within 90 days of the expense date via Maplewood's online portal.

### 1.4 Disability Coverage

**Short-term disability (STD):**
- Begins after 3 consecutive sick days are exhausted
- Pays 75% of base salary for up to 17 weeks
- No waiting period (coverage from Day 1 of employment)

**Long-term disability (LTD):**
- Begins after STD is exhausted (week 18)
- Pays 66.7% of base salary up to a maximum of $12,000 per month
- Coverage begins on Day 31 of employment
- "Own occupation" definition for first 24 months, then "any occupation" thereafter

### 1.5 Life Insurance

- Basic life insurance: 2x annual base salary, paid by PineRidge, from Day 31 of employment
- Optional supplemental life insurance: Available up to 5x annual salary. First $250,000 guaranteed issue (no medical questions) if enrolled within first 30 days. Amounts above $250,000 require medical evidence of insurability.
- Dependent life insurance: $10,000 for spouse, $5,000 per child. Premium: $4.50/month total.
- Accidental death and dismemberment (AD&D): Equal to basic life insurance amount, at no additional cost.

## Section 2: Time Off

### 2.1 Vacation

Vacation accrues from your start date according to your career level:

| Level | Annual Vacation | Accrual Rate |
|-------|----------------|--------------|
| L1–L2 | 15 days (3 weeks) | 1.25 days/month |
| L3 | 20 days (4 weeks) | 1.67 days/month |
| L4–L5 | 25 days (5 weeks) | 2.08 days/month |
| L6 | 30 days (6 weeks) | 2.50 days/month |

Additional accrual for tenure:
- After 5 years: +2 days per year
- After 10 years: +3 additional days per year (total +5 from base)

**Vacation carry-over:** Maximum 5 unused days may be carried into the following year. Carried days must be used by March 31 or they expire. Days beyond the 5-day carry-over limit are forfeited on December 31.

**Vacation blackout periods:** None company-wide, but individual teams may designate up to two (2) blackout weeks per year (e.g., month-end close for Finance). These must be communicated at least 60 days in advance.

**Vacation requests:** Submit via Workday at least 2 weeks before the start date for requests of 3+ days. Shorter absences require 48 hours notice. Managers must respond within 3 business days.

### 2.2 Sick Leave

- 10 paid sick days per calendar year (no accrual — full allocation on January 1, prorated for mid-year hires).
- Unused sick days do not carry over and are not paid out.
- A medical note is required for absences of 4 or more consecutive days.
- Sick leave may be used for your own illness, medical appointments, or caring for a sick dependent (child, spouse, or elderly parent).

### 2.3 Personal Days

- 3 paid personal days per calendar year.
- No reason required. No advance notice required (though courtesy notice is appreciated).
- Cannot be taken consecutively with vacation without manager approval.
- Not paid out on departure. Do not carry over.

### 2.4 Statutory Holidays

PineRidge observes the following paid holidays (2026):

1. New Year's Day — January 1
2. Family Day — February 16
3. Good Friday — April 3
4. Victoria Day — May 18
5. Canada Day — July 1
6. Civic Holiday — August 3
7. Labour Day — September 7
8. National Day for Truth and Reconciliation — September 30
9. Thanksgiving — October 12
10. Remembrance Day — November 11
11. Christmas Day — December 25
12. Boxing Day — December 26

Plus two (2) company-designated days:

- Winter Break Extension — December 24

Employees required to work on a statutory holiday receive 1.5x pay plus a lieu day.

### 2.5 Company Shutdown

PineRidge closes between December 25 and January 1 inclusive each year. This shutdown does not count against your vacation balance. Essential services staff (on-call engineering, security) who work during shutdown receive double time plus a lieu day per day worked.

## Section 3: Parental Leave

### 3.1 Maternity Leave

- Up to 17 weeks of pregnancy leave (per Ontario Employment Standards Act).
- PineRidge tops up Employment Insurance (EI) benefits to 93% of base salary for the full 17 weeks.
- Leave may begin up to 17 weeks before the expected due date. Must begin no later than the date of birth.
- Benefits coverage continues at full company cost during leave.

### 3.2 Parental Leave (Birth or Adoption)

Available to all parents regardless of gender:

- **Standard parental leave:** Up to 35 weeks (63 weeks if extended EI parental elected).
- **PineRidge top-up:** 93% of base salary for the first 18 weeks of parental leave. Remaining weeks are at EI rate only.
- **Adoption:** Same entitlements as parental leave. No distinction between biological and adoptive parents.
- **Surrogacy:** Intended parents are eligible for the same parental leave and top-up.

### 3.3 Return from Parental Leave

- Guaranteed return to the same role or an equivalent role at the same level and compensation.
- Graduated return available: work 60% for the first 4 weeks back, 80% for the next 4 weeks, then full-time. Full salary maintained during graduated return.
- Vacation continues to accrue during leave at the normal rate.
- Performance reviews: any review falling during leave is deferred. Ratings from the pre-leave review carry forward.
- Stock vesting: vesting schedule is paused during unpaid portions of leave and resumes upon return.

### 3.4 Fertility and Family Planning

- 5 paid days per calendar year for fertility treatments, adoption proceedings, or surrogacy appointments. These are in addition to sick leave.
- Fertility treatment coverage under the Enhanced plan (see Section 1.1).
- Pregnancy loss leave: 5 paid days, no documentation required.

## Section 4: Retirement and Financial Benefits

### 4.1 Registered Retirement Savings Plan (RRSP) Matching

- PineRidge matches employee RRSP contributions dollar-for-dollar up to 5% of base salary.
- Matching begins after 90 days of employment (retroactive to Day 1 contributions once vested).
- Vesting: Company match is 50% vested after 1 year, 100% vested after 2 years.
- Contributions are deducted pre-tax from each pay period. You can change your contribution percentage at any time via Workday.

### 4.2 Employee Stock Purchase Plan (ESPP)

- Available after 6 months of employment.
- Contribute up to 10% of base salary per pay period.
- Purchase PineRidge shares at a 15% discount to the lower of the price at the start or end of each 6-month offering period.
- Enrollment windows: January 1 and July 1 each year.
- Shares are purchased on June 30 and December 31.

### 4.3 Annual Bonus

- All employees L2 and above are eligible for an annual performance bonus.
- Target bonus by level:

| Level | Target Bonus (% of base salary) |
|-------|-------------------------------|
| L2 | 5% |
| L3 | 10% |
| L4 | 15% |
| L5 | 20% |
| L6 | 30% |

- Actual payout ranges from 0% to 150% of target based on: individual performance (50% weight), team performance (30% weight), company performance (20% weight).
- Bonuses are paid in March for the prior calendar year.
- You must be employed on the payout date to receive the bonus. Employees who resign before payout date forfeit the bonus (except where prohibited by provincial law).

### 4.4 Referral Bonus

- $3,000 CAD for referring a candidate who is hired and completes 90 days of employment.
- $5,000 CAD for senior roles (L4+).
- No limit on the number of referrals per year.
- Paid on the referred employee's 91st day. Taxed as regular income.
- Not available for referring someone into your own direct reporting chain.

## Section 5: Additional Benefits and Perks

### 5.1 Commuter Benefits

- Monthly transit pass subsidy: up to $150/month for public transit passes (submit receipt via Workday).
- Cycling benefit: $500 annual allocation for bicycle purchase, repair, or accessories.
- Parking: Free parking available at Thunder Bay and Halifax offices. Vancouver office provides a $100/month parking subsidy (limited spots, waitlisted by seniority).

### 5.2 Wellness Program

- Annual wellness spending: $500 per employee (included in HSA — see Section 1.3).
- On-site wellness: Thunder Bay office has a quiet room and weekly yoga sessions (Tuesdays, 12:00 PM).
- Mental health: In addition to plan coverage, employees have access to the Employee Assistance Program (EAP) — free, confidential counseling for employees and household members. Up to 8 sessions per issue per year.
- Burnout prevention: Managers are trained to recognize early signs. Employees may request a temporary workload reduction without stigma.

### 5.3 Home Office Setup

For hybrid and remote employees:

- One-time home office stipend: $750 upon hire (receipts required within 60 days).
- Annual refresh: $250 per year for replacement items.
- Ergonomic assessment: Free virtual ergonomic assessment available through HR. If the assessment recommends equipment (standing desk, ergonomic chair, monitor arm), PineRidge will cover up to $1,200 for recommended items.
- Internet subsidy: $50/month for fully remote employees, $25/month for hybrid employees.

### 5.4 Professional Development

In addition to the $2,500 learning budget (see Employee Handbook Section 4.3):

- Conference attendance: Up to 2 conferences per year. Registration covered by learning budget; travel and accommodation covered separately by your team's operating budget (manager approval required).
- Certification bonuses: Passing a pre-approved professional certification earns a one-time bonus of $500.
- Internal tech talks: Presenting at a PineRidge Lightning Talk earns a $50 gift card.
- Patent filing: $2,000 bonus upon provisional patent filing, additional $3,000 upon grant.

### 5.5 Employee Discounts

- Cell phone plan: 25% discount on Northwind Mobile corporate plans.
- Software subscriptions: Discounted personal licenses for JetBrains, Notion, and 1Password (details on the intranet).
- Local partnerships: Discounts at various Thunder Bay, Vancouver, and Halifax businesses. Full list updated quarterly at perks.pineridge.internal.

## Section 6: Leave of Absence (Non-Parental)

### 6.1 Bereavement Leave

- Immediate family (spouse, child, parent, sibling): 5 paid days.
- Extended family (grandparent, grandchild, in-law, aunt/uncle): 3 paid days.
- Close friend or non-family household member: 2 paid days.
- Additional unpaid leave of up to 2 weeks available upon request. Granted without documentation requirement.
- Travel bereavement: If travel of more than 500 km is required, an additional 2 paid travel days are provided.

### 6.2 Jury Duty

- Full salary maintained for the duration of jury duty (no limit on days).
- Jury duty pay received from the court must be remitted to PineRidge (or the equivalent amount deducted from salary, employee's choice).
- If jury duty extends beyond 4 weeks, a temporary coverage plan is created for your role.

### 6.3 Compassionate Care Leave

- Up to 28 weeks of unpaid job-protected leave to care for a gravely ill family member (per Employment Standards Act).
- PineRidge provides salary top-up to 75% for the first 8 weeks.
- Benefits maintained at full company cost for the duration of the leave.

### 6.4 Personal Leave of Absence

- Unpaid leave of up to 6 months may be requested for personal reasons (travel, education, caregiving, personal projects).
- Requires 30 days written notice and manager + HR approval.
- Benefits may be continued at the employee's full cost during unpaid leave.
- Role is held for leaves up to 3 months. Leaves of 3–6 months: best-effort role preservation; a comparable role is guaranteed upon return.
- Seniority and tenure continue to accrue during approved personal leaves.

### 6.5 Domestic Violence Leave

- Up to 10 paid days and 15 unpaid days per calendar year for employees experiencing domestic or sexual violence.
- No documentation required for the first 5 days. Medical or legal documentation may be requested for days 6–10 (at HR's discretion and handled with strict confidentiality).
- Includes time for: legal proceedings, moving, medical appointments, counseling, safety planning.
- PineRidge will assist with workplace safety planning (e.g., changing work schedule, building access controls) as needed.

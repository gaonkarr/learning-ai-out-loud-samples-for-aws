# PineRidge Solutions — Leave and Time-Off Policy
**Detailed Reference for People Managers and Employees**
**Document ID: PR-LV-2026-007**
**Effective: January 1, 2026**

## Purpose

This document provides detailed procedures and guidelines for all types of leave available to PineRidge employees. It supplements the Benefits Guide (PR-BEN-2026-018) with operational details for requesting, approving, tracking, and returning from leave.

---

## Section 1: Vacation Leave — Detailed Procedures

### 1.1 Requesting Vacation

All vacation requests are submitted through Workday. The approval workflow is:

1. Employee submits request in Workday with desired dates.
2. System checks available balance. If insufficient balance, request is flagged but can still be submitted (see Section 1.4 on negative balances).
3. Direct manager receives notification. Manager has 3 business days to approve or deny.
4. If no response in 3 business days, the request auto-escalates to the skip-level manager.
5. Approved requests automatically block the employee's calendar and update the team availability dashboard.

**Advance notice requirements:**
- 1–2 days: 48 hours notice minimum
- 3–5 days: 2 weeks notice minimum
- 6–10 days: 4 weeks notice minimum
- 11+ consecutive days: 6 weeks notice minimum

Requests that don't meet the notice requirement may still be approved at the manager's discretion but are not guaranteed.

### 1.2 Vacation Approval Guidelines (for Managers)

Managers should approve vacation requests unless:

- The absence would leave a critical function uncovered (define backup plan first)
- It falls within a team-designated blackout period (communicated 60+ days in advance)
- More than 40% of the team has already been approved off for the same dates
- The employee has a documented active PIP and the absence would interfere with deliverables

Denial must include a written reason in Workday. Employees may request reconsideration through HR if they feel a denial is unreasonable.

### 1.3 Maximum Consecutive Absence

- Standard maximum: 15 consecutive business days (3 calendar weeks) without VP approval.
- Extended vacation: Requests for 16+ consecutive days require VP approval and a documented coverage plan.
- Sabbatical-length absence (4+ weeks): See Section 7 for Personal Leave of Absence provisions.
- No employee may be absent for more than 6 consecutive weeks under vacation leave alone.

### 1.4 Negative Vacation Balance

Employees may go up to 3 days negative on their vacation balance in exceptional circumstances (e.g., pre-planned trip booked before offer letter limitations were known). Requirements:

- Manager approval
- Written acknowledgment that if the employee departs before the balance recovers, the negative days will be deducted from final pay at the daily rate.
- Cannot go negative more than once per calendar year.

### 1.5 Vacation Payout on Departure

- All accrued, unused vacation is paid out in the final paycheck at the employee's regular daily rate.
- Vacation carry-over days from the prior year are included in the payout.
- Negative balances are deducted from the final paycheck.

## Section 2: Sick Leave — Detailed Procedures

### 2.1 Reporting a Sick Day

- Notify your manager via Slack, email, or text message before your scheduled start time (or as soon as reasonably possible).
- Enter the absence in Workday within 48 hours of returning.
- For absences of 1–3 days: no documentation required.
- For absences of 4+ consecutive days: a medical note confirming fitness to return is required before the employee resumes work. The note need not disclose the diagnosis, only confirm the absence was medically necessary and the employee is cleared to return.

### 2.2 Sick Leave and Patterns

HR may request a conversation (not disciplinary) if patterns emerge:

- 3 or more single-day absences on Mondays or Fridays within a rolling 3-month period
- Sick days consistently adjacent to weekends or holidays
- Total sick leave usage exceeding 80% of annual allocation by mid-year

These conversations are exploratory, not punitive. The goal is to identify if the employee needs additional support (accommodation, workload adjustment, mental health resources).

### 2.3 Sick Leave for Dependents

Employees may use up to 5 of their 10 sick days per year to care for a sick dependent (child under 18, spouse, or elderly parent/grandparent for whom the employee is primary caregiver). If more than 5 days are needed for dependent care, employees may:

- Use personal days
- Request unpaid Family Responsibility Leave (up to 3 days per occurrence, job-protected under ESA)
- Request a temporary schedule accommodation

### 2.4 Long-term Illness Transition

If an illness extends beyond 10 sick days:

- Days 1–3: Regular sick leave
- Days 4–10: Remaining sick leave allocation
- Days 11–17: Short-term disability at 75% salary (see Benefits Guide Section 1.4)
- Day 18+: Transition to STD formal claim process with Maplewood Benefits Corp
- Week 18+: Long-term disability if applicable

HR initiates a check-in call on Day 5 of any continuous absence to discuss support options and anticipated timeline.

## Section 3: Parental Leave — Detailed Procedures

### 3.1 Notification and Planning

- Notify your manager and HR at least 4 weeks before the intended start of leave (8 weeks recommended for coverage planning).
- Submit a Parental Leave Request form in Workday, indicating:
  - Expected start date
  - Intended duration
  - Whether you plan to use standard (35 weeks) or extended (63 weeks) parental leave
  - Whether you want to split leave with a partner (if applicable)

### 3.2 Before You Leave

- Complete a knowledge transfer document and share with your coverage team.
- Set Slack status and email auto-reply indicating you are on leave and who to contact.
- Decide whether you want to be contacted for emergencies only, not at all, or for optional social updates. Document this preference in Workday and communicate it to your manager. Managers must respect this preference.
- Ensure your direct reports (if any) have a clear interim reporting line.

### 3.3 During Leave

- You will not be contacted for work purposes unless you have opted in to emergency-only contact and a genuine emergency arises (defined as: risk of data loss, security breach, or loss of a client contract worth >$500K).
- Benefits continue. See Benefits Guide Section 3 for top-up details.
- You retain access to Slack and email for social purposes if desired, but there is no expectation to check either.
- Vacation continues to accrue. Sick leave does not accrue during unpaid portions.

### 3.4 Return Planning

- Notify HR at least 4 weeks before your intended return date.
- If you wish to change your return date (earlier or later than originally planned), provide at least 2 weeks notice.
- Options for return:
  - **Full return:** Resume full-time schedule immediately.
  - **Graduated return:** 60% for first 4 weeks, 80% for next 4 weeks, then full-time. Full salary maintained. Only available for leaves of 12+ weeks.
  - **Reduced schedule return:** Request a temporary or permanent schedule reduction (e.g., 4-day week). Subject to manager and HR approval. Compensation prorated.

### 3.5 Parental Leave and Performance

- No performance review will be scheduled during leave or within 4 weeks of return.
- The pre-leave performance rating carries forward. You cannot be disadvantaged in calibration due to absence.
- Promotion eligibility: time on parental leave counts toward time-in-role requirements for promotion.

## Section 4: Bereavement Leave — Detailed Procedures

### 4.1 Reporting Bereavement

- Notify your manager by any convenient means. No formal process is required on Day 1.
- Enter the absence in Workday within one week of returning (HR will assist if needed).
- No documentation is required for bereavement leave. PineRidge operates on trust.

### 4.2 Entitlements Summary

| Relationship | Paid Days | Travel Days (if >500km travel) |
|-------------|-----------|-------------------------------|
| Spouse/partner | 5 | +2 |
| Child (including stillbirth) | 5 | +2 |
| Parent | 5 | +2 |
| Sibling | 5 | +2 |
| Grandparent | 3 | +2 |
| Grandchild | 3 | +2 |
| In-law (parent, sibling) | 3 | +2 |
| Aunt, uncle, cousin | 3 | +2 |
| Close friend / chosen family | 2 | +2 |

### 4.3 Extended Bereavement

If additional time is needed beyond the paid entitlement:

- Up to 2 additional weeks of unpaid leave, no questions asked.
- Sick leave may be used if grief is affecting the employee's health (self-assessed, no note required).
- EAP services are available immediately (up to 8 sessions per issue).
- Manager should proactively check in at 2 weeks and 4 weeks post-return, but not pressure an early return.

### 4.4 Pregnancy and Infant Loss

A specific provision applies:

- Miscarriage: 5 paid days, applicable to the pregnant person and their partner.
- Stillbirth: 5 paid days of bereavement plus eligibility for up to 17 weeks of medical leave if the pregnant person requires physical recovery.
- NICU stay: If a newborn requires NICU hospitalization, parental leave top-up begins on the date of hospital discharge, not birth. Sick leave or STD covers the NICU period.

## Section 5: Unpaid Leaves — Job Protection and Benefits

### 5.1 General Principles

For all unpaid leaves (beyond statutory minimums):

- Benefits continuation: Employee may continue group benefits by paying the full premium (employee + employer share). HR provides a premium schedule before leave begins.
- Seniority: Continues to accrue for unpaid leaves up to 6 months.
- RRSP matching: Paused during unpaid periods. No retroactive catch-up upon return.
- ESPP: Participation paused during leave. May re-enroll at the next enrollment window after return.

### 5.2 Statutory vs. Company Leaves

Some leaves are legally protected (statutory) and some are PineRidge-specific. Key distinctions:

| Leave Type | Statutory Minimum | PineRidge Enhancement |
|-----------|-------------------|----------------------|
| Pregnancy leave | 17 weeks unpaid, job-protected | 93% salary top-up |
| Parental leave | 35 or 63 weeks unpaid, job-protected | 93% top-up for first 18 weeks |
| Bereavement | 2 unpaid days (ON ESA) | 2–5 paid days + travel days |
| Family responsibility | 3 unpaid days/year | Dependent sick days from own allocation |
| Domestic violence | 10 days (5 paid, 5 unpaid under ESA) | 10 paid + 15 unpaid |
| Personal leave | None (not statutory) | Up to 6 months (company policy) |

### 5.3 Leave Stacking

Employees may combine multiple leave types in sequence. Example: an employee experiencing pregnancy complications might use:

1. Sick leave (10 days)
2. Short-term disability (17 weeks)
3. Pregnancy leave (17 weeks, with top-up)
4. Parental leave (35 weeks, first 18 with top-up)

Total possible protected absence in this scenario: approximately 16 months. HR will work with the employee to map the optimal sequence.

## Section 6: Holidays and Shutdown — Operational Details

### 6.1 Working on a Statutory Holiday

If business needs require work on a statutory holiday:

- Only volunteers are asked first. If insufficient volunteers, the manager may assign based on rotation.
- Compensation: 1.5x hourly rate for all hours worked PLUS a paid lieu day to be taken within 3 months.
- On-call (but not called in): $150 flat on-call stipend for the holiday. If called in, the full 1.5x + lieu day applies.

### 6.2 Religious and Cultural Observances

PineRidge recognizes that employees observe different holidays. If a religious or cultural observance falls on a workday that is not a company holiday:

- Employees may use a personal day or vacation day.
- Alternatively, employees may request a schedule swap (work on a company holiday in exchange for a different day off). Subject to manager approval and business coverage.
- No employee may be required to justify which observances they celebrate.

### 6.3 Winter Shutdown Details

The December 25 – January 1 shutdown:

- All offices closed. No meetings may be scheduled.
- On-call engineering rotation continues (skeleton crew of 2 per shift, volunteer-first). Compensation: double time + lieu day per day worked.
- Customer-facing SLAs revert to "best effort" response during shutdown. Clients are notified in early December.
- Employees do not need to submit a Workday request for shutdown days. They are automatically applied.

## Section 7: Personal Leave of Absence — Extended

### 7.1 Eligibility

- Minimum 18 months of continuous employment.
- No active PIP or disciplinary action.
- Must have used fewer than 10 cumulative days of unapproved absence in the preceding 12 months.

### 7.2 Duration Options

- Short personal leave: 1–4 weeks. Manager approval only.
- Medium personal leave: 1–3 months. Manager + HR approval.
- Extended personal leave: 3–6 months. Manager + HR + VP approval.
- Sabbatical (new in 2026): After 7 years of continuous service, employees are eligible for a 4-week paid sabbatical. Not combinable with other leave types. Must be taken as one continuous block.

### 7.3 Role Preservation

- Leaves up to 3 months: Same role held for the employee.
- Leaves of 3–6 months: A comparable role at the same level and compensation band is guaranteed. The exact team or project may differ.
- Sabbatical: Same role guaranteed (4 weeks is too short for a backfill).

### 7.4 Examples of Personal Leave Use

PineRidge employees have used personal leave for:

- Travel (extended trips abroad)
- Caring for aging parents during a health crisis
- Writing a book or pursuing a creative project
- Completing an academic program
- Mental health recovery and extended rest
- Relocating to a new city (ahead of a remote role)

No reason is "more valid" than another. The approval process considers coverage feasibility, not the merits of the reason.

## Section 8: Attendance Expectations and Accommodations

### 8.1 Unexcused Absence

An absence is considered unexcused if:

- No notification is provided to the manager before or during the absence
- No leave request is submitted in Workday within 48 hours of return
- The absence does not fall under any leave type described in this document

Three unexcused absences within a rolling 12-month period may result in a formal conversation with HR. Six unexcused absences may initiate disciplinary action.

### 8.2 Flexible Accommodations

Employees who need ongoing flexibility beyond what standard policies provide should discuss accommodations with their manager and HR. Examples:

- Chronic illness requiring unpredictable half-days off
- Caregiving responsibilities that vary week to week
- Mental health conditions requiring periodic reduced workloads

Formal accommodations are documented in a confidential agreement. They are reviewed every 6 months or when circumstances change, whichever comes first.

### 8.3 Return-to-Work After Extended Absence

For any absence exceeding 4 consecutive weeks (regardless of type):

- HR schedules a return-to-work conversation in the week before return.
- Topics: workspace readiness, technology access, any changes to team or process during absence, emotional readiness.
- Manager creates a 2-week re-onboarding plan (lighter meeting load, catch-up sessions, no deliverables in week 1).
- IT verifies all access remains active and re-provisions if needed.
- Buddy system: a colleague is assigned to help with informal catch-up (similar to new employee onboarding buddy).

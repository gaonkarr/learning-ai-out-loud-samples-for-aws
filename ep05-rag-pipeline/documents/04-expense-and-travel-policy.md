# PineRidge Solutions — Expense and Travel Policy
**Document ID: PR-EXP-2026-003**
**Effective: January 1, 2026**
**Applies to: All employees incurring business expenses or traveling on company business**

---

## Policy Statement

PineRidge reimburses employees for reasonable business expenses incurred while performing their duties. This policy establishes what is reimbursable, the approval process, and documentation requirements. The guiding principle: spend company money as thoughtfully as you would your own.

---

## Section 1: Expense Submission Process

### 1.1 How to Submit

All expense claims are submitted through Expensify (integrated with Workday).

1. Photograph or upload the receipt.
2. Categorize the expense (meal, travel, accommodation, supplies, etc.).
3. Enter the amount and currency.
4. Tag the appropriate cost center / project code (Engineering, Marketing, G&A, or specific client code).
5. Add a brief business justification for expenses over $100 (one sentence is sufficient).
6. Submit the report.

### 1.2 Approval Workflow

| Expense Amount | Approver |
|---------------|----------|
| Under $250 | Auto-approved (receipts still required) |
| $250 – $1,000 | Direct manager |
| $1,001 – $5,000 | Director or VP of the cost center |
| Over $5,000 | CFO pre-approval required before expense is incurred |

### 1.3 Submission Deadlines

- Expenses must be submitted within 30 calendar days of being incurred.
- Expenses submitted 31–60 days late require manager approval and a brief written explanation.
- Expenses submitted more than 60 days after being incurred will not be reimbursed except in extraordinary circumstances (prolonged medical absence, etc.) approved by the CFO.

### 1.4 Reimbursement Timeline

- Standard processing: 5–7 business days from approval.
- Payment method: Direct deposit to the same account as payroll.
- Currency: All expenses are reimbursed in CAD. Foreign currency expenses are converted at the Bank of Canada exchange rate on the date the expense was incurred.

### 1.5 Receipt Requirements

- Required for all expenses $25 or above.
- Credit card statements alone are not sufficient (they don't show itemization).
- If a receipt is lost, submit a signed Lost Receipt Declaration form (available in Expensify). Maximum of 3 lost receipt declarations per calendar year.
- Digital receipts (email confirmations, screenshots of booking confirmations) are acceptable.

## Section 2: Travel Policy

### 2.1 When Travel is Required

Travel is approved when it serves a clear business purpose:

- Client meetings that cannot be conducted virtually
- Conferences and events (within the $2,500 learning budget, or team operating budget)
- Inter-office collaboration (Thunder Bay ↔ Vancouver ↔ Halifax)
- Company events (all-hands, team offsites)

All travel must be pre-approved by your manager before booking. For international travel, VP approval is required regardless of cost.

### 2.2 Booking

- **Air travel:** Book economy class for flights under 5 hours. Premium economy permitted for flights 5–8 hours. Business class permitted for flights over 8 hours or with VP approval.
- **Preferred airline:** No preferred carrier. Employees should book the most cost-effective option within 20% of the lowest available fare at time of booking. Loyalty program preferences are acceptable within this margin.
- **Advance booking:** Book flights at least 14 days in advance when possible. Last-minute bookings (under 7 days) require written justification.
- **Hotels:** Up to $225 per night in Canadian cities, up to $300 per night in major international cities (NYC, London, SF, Sydney, Tokyo). Government rate or corporate rate should be requested where available.
- **Car rental:** Intermediate/standard class vehicles. Insurance must be purchased through the rental company (PineRidge corporate coverage does not extend to rental vehicles). Fuel expenses are reimbursable.
- **Personal vehicle:** Reimbursed at $0.62 per km. Mileage log required (start point, end point, total km, business purpose). Maximum reimbursement per trip: equivalent to the cheapest available flight for the same route.
- **Rail:** Preferred over air for trips under 4 hours by train (e.g., Toronto to Ottawa, Vancouver to Seattle). Business class permitted on VIA Rail and Amtrak.

### 2.3 Accommodation Alternatives

- Airbnb/VRBO: Permitted for stays of 4+ nights where the nightly rate is below the hotel policy limit. Must be an entire unit (not shared accommodations). Receipt required.
- Staying with friends/family: Employees who forgo a hotel while on business travel may claim a $75/night hosting stipend (no receipt required) to compensate the host.

### 2.4 Per Diem Meals While Traveling

Instead of submitting individual meal receipts while on business travel, employees may elect a per diem:

| Meal | Per Diem Rate |
|------|--------------|
| Breakfast | $20 |
| Lunch | $30 |
| Dinner | $55 |
| Full day | $105 |

Alternatively, employees may submit actual receipts. If submitting actual receipts, reasonable judgment applies (see Section 3 for meal guidelines). You cannot mix per diem and actual receipts on the same day.

Alcohol with meals while traveling: up to $20 per meal occasion (wine or beer with dinner). Spirits, cocktails above $20, and standalone bar tabs are not reimbursable.

### 2.5 Travel Days

- Travel time during regular business hours counts as work time.
- Weekend travel required for a Monday business engagement: one lieu day granted (to be taken within 30 days).
- Red-eye flights: If a red-eye is taken to save a hotel night, the following day may begin at noon without using leave.

## Section 3: Business Meals and Entertainment

### 3.1 Client Meals

- Reimbursable when there is a clear business discussion.
- Include on the receipt (or in the expense note): client company name, attendees, and brief purpose.
- Reasonable limits:
  - Lunch: up to $50 per person
  - Dinner: up to $100 per person
  - Coffee/informal meeting: up to $20 per person
- Alcohol: up to 2 drinks per person. The total alcohol portion of a client meal should not exceed 30% of the bill.

### 3.2 Team Meals

- Manager-approved team meals (celebrations, offsites, project milestones): up to $75 per person.
- Frequency: up to once per month per team.
- Must include all team members present (no excluding individuals).
- Can be submitted by the manager or any team member designated to pay.

### 3.3 Internal One-on-Ones

- Reimbursable up to $30 when conducted outside the office (coffee shop, lunch, etc.).
- Common use: manager/report 1:1s, cross-team relationship building, mentoring sessions.
- Maximum: 4 reimbursed 1:1 meals per month.

## Section 4: Office and Equipment Expenses

### 4.1 Office Supplies

- Standard supplies (notebooks, pens, sticky notes, etc.): Order through the Staples corporate account. No personal reimbursement for items available through the corporate account.
- Specialized supplies not available through corporate account: Submit receipt via Expensify. Auto-approved under $50.

### 4.2 Software and Subscriptions

- Approved software list is maintained by IT at software.pineridge.internal.
- Software on the approved list: Purchase and expense. Auto-approved under $100/year per subscription.
- Software NOT on the approved list: Requires IT review before purchase (submit via #it-requests). Typical review time: 3 business days.
- No personal software licenses may be charged to PineRidge.

### 4.3 Home Office Equipment

(See Benefits Guide Section 5.3 for home office stipend details.)

For equipment beyond the stipend:

- Ergonomic assessment recommendation required (see Benefits Guide).
- Approved items shipped directly from vendor when possible (charged to PineRidge, no personal expense).
- Employee-purchased items: Submit receipt, reimbursed within standard timeline.
- All company-funded home office equipment valued over $250 must be returned upon departure.

### 4.4 Books and Learning Materials

- Physical or digital books related to your role: Reimbursable under the $2,500 learning budget (no separate approval needed for individual items under $75).
- Subscriptions (O'Reilly, Pluralsight, etc.): Annual subscriptions count against learning budget. If team-wide access is needed, contact L&D for a group license (does not count against individual budgets).

## Section 5: Non-Reimbursable Expenses

The following are explicitly not reimbursable regardless of circumstances:

- Personal travel or vacation extensions added to business trips (the personal portion of flights, extra hotel nights, etc.)
- Traffic violations, parking tickets, or towing fees
- Childcare or pet care during business travel (exception: emergency dependent care for unplanned travel extensions, up to $150/day, manager approval)
- Laundry and dry cleaning (exception: trips exceeding 5 consecutive nights — up to $30/day for laundry)
- In-room entertainment (movies, minibar)
- Airline lounge memberships (exception: employees traveling more than 24 days/year may expense one domestic lounge membership up to $500/year)
- First-class air travel (unless medically required with documentation)
- Charitable donations (PineRidge has a separate corporate giving program)
- Personal cell phone plans (covered under employee discount, not expense policy)
- Gym or fitness expenses while traveling (covered under HSA wellness spending)
- Gifts to other employees exceeding $50 (see Section 6 for recognition guidelines)

## Section 6: Recognition and Gifts

### 6.1 Employee Recognition

- Managers may expense recognition gifts (gift cards, small items) up to $50 per employee per occasion.
- Maximum: 4 recognition gifts per direct report per year.
- All gift cards are taxable benefits and must be reported to Payroll (Expensify handles this automatically when categorized as "recognition").

### 6.2 Client Gifts

- Holiday or thank-you gifts to clients: up to $150 per gift, per client, per year.
- Must be pre-approved by the account manager or VP of Sales.
- No cash or cash-equivalent gifts to clients.
- All client gifts must be logged in Salesforce for compliance tracking.

### 6.3 Life Event Acknowledgments

- Team-organized collections for life events (wedding, baby, bereavement): PineRidge will match team contributions up to $200 per event. Submit via manager's expense report.
- Flowers or food delivery for bereavement, illness, or new baby: up to $100 charged directly to the team cost center.

## Section 7: Corporate Credit Cards

### 7.1 Eligibility

Corporate credit cards are issued to:

- Employees who travel more than 6 times per year
- Managers with team expense authority
- Any employee upon manager request (for specific business needs)

### 7.2 Rules

- Corporate cards are for business expenses only. Personal charges are prohibited.
- Unauthorized personal charges must be repaid within 5 business days of discovery. Repeated violations result in card revocation and potential disciplinary action.
- Monthly statements must be reconciled in Expensify within 10 business days of the statement date.
- Credit limit: $5,000 standard. Higher limits available with CFO approval.
- Card must be returned (or confirmed destroyed) upon departure.

### 7.3 Lost or Stolen Cards

- Report immediately to the issuing bank (number on back of card) AND to finance@pineridge.internal.
- Finance will verify no unauthorized charges and issue a replacement within 5 business days.
- If unauthorized charges appear, Finance initiates the dispute process. The employee is not personally liable for fraudulent charges reported within 24 hours.

---

## Section 8: Expense Audits

### 8.1 Routine Audits

- Finance conducts random audits of 10% of expense reports each month.
- Audit checks: appropriate categorization, valid receipts, policy compliance, accurate business justification.
- Findings are shared with the employee and their manager. Most issues are simple corrections.

### 8.2 Flags and Escalation

- Consistent over-spending relative to policy limits triggers a conversation with Finance.
- Missing receipts on more than 20% of claims in a quarter triggers a process review.
- Suspected fraud (fabricated receipts, personal expenses disguised as business, duplicate claims) is escalated to the VP People and Legal. This may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

### 8.3 Clawback

If an expense is later determined to be non-compliant:

- Employee is notified and given 10 business days to provide additional justification or correct the claim.
- If not resolved, the amount is deducted from the next payroll cycle with written notice.
- Amounts over $500 may be arranged as a repayment plan (deducted over multiple pay periods).
